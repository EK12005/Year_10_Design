Why Should You Listen to Music?


A Website on the Importance of Auditory Stimulation


Introduction



Since ancient times, music has been a large part of human culture and expression. Many studies have been done on the
benefits of learning to play an instrument and read music
early in one's life.
However, the consumption and listening of music is often overlooked, and is therefore not seen as helpful as the actual
playing of an instrument. This website will explain why that is not the case, and hopefully give you an introduction
to the vast world of musical listening.

Physical Benefits



There are more than a fair share of studies that discuss the effects of listening to music on athletic or training performance.
A 2003 study found that when listening to high-tempo music, participants biked a 10 km run approximately 22 seconds faster than those who did not listen to anything, signifying how fast tempo and rhythm could be used as a motivator for physical exercise.
Another study found that when listening to music, a faster tempo or louder sound resulted in participants running at a faster pace on their treadmills.

Mental Benefits



There is lots of evidence that music is beneficial to mental health.
For example, a 2011 study found that dopamine levels increased by 9% at a listener's emotional climax to an instrumental song, showcasing the correlation between the important neurotransmitter and the consumption of music.
In addition, soothing and relaxing music has been proven to aid sleep by triggering the release of neurotransmitters like serotonin and oxytocin.
